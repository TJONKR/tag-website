02 — Name 01

Vibe Club.

The energy is the point
The name with the most momentum. The 8 rules of Vibeclub already exist. Hackathons have been run under it. The Fight Club parallel is built into the DNA. "Vibe" captures the intangible energy. "Club" means you're in or you're not. Together: a place with a specific energy, and you belong or you don't. It captures joy + intensity without being corporate. Scales from room to movement.
Why it works
Already has cultural infrastructure. The 8 rules, the hackathons, the Fight Club DNA. Clean open space — no competitors in builder/tech. Sounds fun. Sounds exclusive.
The risk
First impression might be nightlife. Could sound unserious to institutional partners. Doesn't describe what happens inside. "Vibe" might age poorly as cultural language shifts.
Bar test: "I'm at Vibe Club" — fun, exclusive, energetic. But: does Techleap put this in a press release?
03 — Name 02

Ground Zero.

The epicenter
The origin point. Where everything starts. Ground zero is the epicenter — the place where the impact happens first. It directly captures the "cultural layer underneath" positioning. You are ground zero of the European builder movement. It has urgency and scale. It sounds important.
Why it works
Most powerful-sounding name on the list. Captures the origin/epicenter positioning perfectly. Has urgency and scale.
The risk
The 9/11 association is unavoidable, especially internationally. Connotation is destruction, not creation. Serious and heavy. Zero joy factor.
Bar test: "I'm at Ground Zero" — intense and important. But could raise eyebrows. The baggage is real.
04 — Name 03

Basecamp.

Before the summit
The communal gathering point before the climb. Adventure, ambition, patience — you're not at the top yet, you're getting ready. Almost a perfect metaphor for the pre-institutional layer. In gaming, "base" is where you spawn, regroup, gear up. It's communal. It's physical.
Why it works
Perfect metaphor for the upstream positioning. Communal, physical, adventurous. Gaming "base" connection works.
The risk
37signals' Basecamp (250k+ customers) is a dealbreaker — every builder knows it. The metaphor implies leaving. Doesn't feel Amsterdam.
Bar test: "I'm at Basecamp" — sounds like you're using a PM tool. The collision kills it.
05 — Name 04

Scratch.

Built from nothing
"From scratch" is universally understood. But the history goes deeper. "The scratch" was the starting line scratched into the ground in boxing. Coming "up to scratch" meant you were ready to fight. That connects to the Fight Club DNA. In DJing, scratching is Amsterdam music culture. In coding, MIT's Scratch is where millions learned to build. The word sits at the intersection of creation, music, sport, and code.
Why it works
Strongest philosophical foundation. The one-liner is instant: "It's where you build from scratch." Boxing, DJing, coding connections all land.
The risk
Literal meaning isn't glamorous. "I'm at Scratch" sounds odd. MIT's Scratch exists. Doesn't immediately communicate community.
Bar test: "I'm at Scratch" — unexpected. Makes people ask. Then: "it's where you build from scratch." That click is powerful.
06 — Name 05

Co-lab.

Collaboration + Laboratory
Collaboration and laboratory in one word. "I'm in the lab" is already how builders talk — music, science, hacker culture. Collective energy ("co-") plus experimentation energy ("lab"). Everyone gets it immediately. But that's also the problem: it's expected. Descriptive, safe, functional. Never raises an eyebrow but also never makes someone say "what the fuck, that's amazing."
Why it works
Immediately understood. "The lab" has culture credibility. Works as a physical place. The hyphen gives visual identity.
The risk
Dozens of coworking spaces already use it worldwide. Generic. No cultural edge. Name collision is guaranteed.
Bar test: "I'm at the Co-lab" — clear and functional. Doesn't turn heads. Nobody asks what it is.
07 — Name 06

Builders
Collective.

The most accurate description
"Collective" implies independence within a group — everyone doing their own thing as part of something bigger. Guild energy. Workers banding together as equals. "Builders" tells you who belongs. It has the Vrijmetselaars (Freemasons / "free builders") parallel. It directly answers "what is this?"
Why it works
Most accurate description of what this is. "Collective" carries independence, equality, shared purpose. Vrijmetselaars adds Dutch depth.
The risk
builderscollective.xyz already exists — European founders/VC community. Direct overlap. Generic. Nobody puts this on a hoodie. Shortens to "TBC" = "to be confirmed."
Bar test: "I'm part of the Builders Collective" — clear, communal. A bit long. A bit corporate. A bit forgettable.
08 — Name 07

The New
Foundation.

The base layer, rebuilt
Institutional but rebellious. "New" says we're replacing whatever came before. "Foundation" says this is serious infrastructure — the base layer everything else gets built on. The cultural layer positioning baked right into the name. Same energy as "The New School" in New York — an institution defined by being the alternative to the establishment.
Why it works
The positioning is the name. Institutional credibility built in. Sounds important. Partners love this language.
The risk
Long — becomes "TNF" or "The Foundation." Could mean nonprofit or charity. "The Foundation" alone is generic. No joy, no playfulness.
Bar test: "I'm at The New Foundation" — sounds important. But three words. Day to day it shortens to something generic.
09 — Name 08

AIGEN.

The AI Generation
Say it out loud. AI-gen. The AI generation. Five letters that immediately tell you what era this belongs to, what these people build with, and why this moment matters.

It's 2026. AI isn't a feature anymore — it's the medium. The people building with it right now aren't just early adopters. They're the generation that defines what gets built next. AIGEN says: this is where that generation gathers.

Short. Punchy. Tech-native. It could sit next to Vercel, Figma, Anthropic on a partner page and not blink. It sounds like a company with weight — because it is one.
09b — The Hidden Layer

Eigen.

"Je eigen ding doen"
But there's a layer underneath that only the right people catch.

In Dutch, "eigen" means own, self, one's own. "Je eigen ding doen" — doing your own thing. That's not just a phrase. That's literally the philosophy of this community.

The Eigenvalue in linear algebra — the vector that doesn't change direction under transformation. The thing that stays true to itself. Eigenlijk — "actually," "truly." What something really is beneath the surface.

So AIGEN carries two stories at once: AI generation on the surface for the world. Deeply Dutch "eigen" underneath for the people who belong. A public name with a private meaning. The insiders know.
09c — The Verdict

AIGEN.

Where it lands
Why it works
Positions you in the AI builder lane at exactly the right moment. The Dutch "eigen" rewards insiders. Sounds premium and tech-forward. "AIGEN Amsterdam" has institutional weight. Hoodie-ready. Investor-deck-ready.
The risk
Very AI-specific. What happens when the community evolves beyond AI? You're locked into a category. There are a hundred AI-names in 2026. The "eigen" layer only works for Dutch speakers — everyone else just hears "AI."
The brand system
AIGEN Labs, AIGEN Amsterdam, AIGEN Night. The word generates a tech-company namespace. But it doesn't generate verbs. You can't "AIGEN someone in." The language is clean but static.
The bar test
"I'm at AIGEN" — sounds like a company that matters. Instantly tech-credible. "What's AIGEN?" "AI builder community. Amsterdam." Two sentences. Done.
12 — Name 09

TAG.

Three letters. Infinite layers.
Three letters. One word everyone already knows. But underneath — layer after layer after layer.

A graffiti tag is your mark on the world. You were here. You made something. Street culture. Very Amsterdam. "Tag, you're it" — an invitation, a challenge, a dare. You can't just watch. You're in. Tag someone in — collaboration. Bringing someone into the fight. Dog tags — identity. You belong to something.

"Tag us" — the native language of building in public. You ship something, you tag us. You share what you made, you tag us. It's how the community grows organically: every post, every demo, every launch becomes a signal. Tag us and we amplify you.

HTML tags. Git tags. The literal building blocks of everything we make. Said fast, TAG sounds like "tech." And it behaves like one: short, punchy, impossible to misspell.
12b — The Amsterdam Connection

Leave your tag.

Street culture meets builder culture
Amsterdam is a graffiti city. Always has been. The walls, the bridges, the trains — people have been leaving their marks here for decades. A tag isn't vandalism. It's proof you exist. It says: I was here, and I made something you can't ignore.

That's exactly what builders do. You ship something. You put it in the world. You leave your tag. The parallel isn't forced — it's native. The energy of a graffiti writer and the energy of someone shipping at 2am are the same energy. Creation as an act of defiance. Making something out of nothing and daring people to notice.

"TAG Amsterdam" doesn't just describe where we are. It sounds like we own the city. Like the city is ours to build on.
12c — The Hidden Acronym

To Achieve Greatness.

The layer people discover
You don't lead with this. You don't put it on the website. It's the thing people find out after they've been around for a while — and then it clicks.

To Achieve Greatness. Not dreaming about greatness. Not talking about greatness. Achieving it. That's a verb. That's action. That's the build-first philosophy compressed into three letters.

The best names have a hidden layer that rewards curiosity. When someone discovers what TAG stands for, they don't just learn a fact — they feel something. The ambition was there all along. Hidden in plain sight.
12d — The Brand System

TAG.

The language it generates
The best test of a name: does it create language? TAG doesn't just name the community. It builds an entire verbal system around it.
Events & Programs
TAG Night — weekly demos and drinks. TAG Team — collaborative build sessions. TAG Drop — launches, when someone ships. TAG Line — the newsletter.
Identity
Tagged — alumni, people who've been through it. Tag In — the invitation, the onboarding. "What's your tag?" — what are you building?
Verbal Hooks
"Tag, you're it." — the challenge. "Leave your tag." — ship something, make your mark. "Tag us." — share what you built, we amplify. "Tag in." — join, collaborate, step up.
The Domain
tag.amsterdam — the .amsterdam TLD exists, low adoption, likely available. Clean. Ownable. Says exactly where this lives.
12e — The Verdict

TAG.

Where it lands
Why it works
Clean, short, impossible to forget. Graffiti energy is native to Amsterdam. The brand system writes itself. Verbal hooks are endless. Scales from a room to a movement. Sounds like something that already matters.
The risk
"Tag" alone can feel small — price tags, luggage tags. Graffiti needs to be shown, not explained. TAG Heuer exists (different lane). "To Achieve Greatness" risks feeling motivational-poster if overused.
Bar test: "I'm at TAG" — sounds like a code name, like a place that matters. "TAG Amsterdam" — clean and strong. "What's TAG?" "To Achieve Greatness. Builder community." Instant.
