# TAG Amsterdam — Continuation Prompt

## Context
This continues a naming and positioning session for a builder community in Amsterdam. The full conversation is available in past chats — search for "cultural layer naming positioning TAG" to find it.

## Who I am
Tijs, founder of Lerai (AI automation agency, Netherlands). Building a physical builder community/space in Amsterdam. Currently ~30 builders at HQ, 2,000+ in broader network, 51 projects shipped, 60 events/year, 6 hackathons/year. Partners: Techleap (Dutch government-backed scaler), Norsken (impact fund), AI AM.

## The positioning (locked in)
"We're the cultural layer underneath the startup ecosystem. We attract the obsessed, creative, talented people before they're ready for their next step. We're where the energy is."

We're NOT an accelerator, incubator, coworking space, or networking event. We're a permanent hackathon — the energy, competition, fun, and shipping culture of a hackathon as a daily reality. We're the upstream of the startup ecosystem: where interesting people are before they're fundable.

The pitch to partners: "You fund companies. We cultivate the people who start them. We're the talent pipeline you can't build yourself."

The European thesis: Europe has the talent but lacks the irrational belief it deserves to win. We're the hype man Europe is missing.

## The name: TAG — To Achieve Greatness
After evaluating 7 candidates (Vibe Club, Ground Zero, Basecamp, Scratch, Co-lab, Builders Collective, TAG), TAG is the frontrunner. Here's why:

**Layers of meaning:**
- Graffiti tag — your mark on the world, street culture, very Amsterdam
- "Tag, you're it" — invitation, challenge, playful competition
- Tag someone in — collaboration, bringing people into the project
- Dog tag — identity, belonging
- HTML/Git tags — code culture
- TAG sounds like "tech" when you say it out loud
- "To Achieve Greatness" — hidden acronym, ambition + action

**Brand system explored:**
- TAG Night — weekly demos and drinks
- TAG Team — collaborative build sessions
- Tagged — alumni, people who've been through it
- Tag In — the invitation/onboarding
- TAG Drop — launches, when someone ships
- TAG Line — the newsletter

**Verbal hooks:**
- "Tag, you're it." — the challenge
- "Leave your tag." — ship something, make your mark
- "Tag in." — join, collaborate
- "What's your tag?" — what are you building

**Domain:** tag.amsterdam is the dream domain (.amsterdam TLD exists, low adoption, likely available)

**Visual directions explored:** Three HTML mockups were created:
1. Street — dark, gritty, graffiti energy, grain overlay, spray-paint red accent
2. Clean — warm off-white, italic serif (Instrument Serif), F.inc-inspired editorial elegance
3. Bold — brutalist grid, thick borders, blue accent, Syne font, institutional weight with energy

## Hackathon roadmap
- **March 2026:** 100-150 person flagship hackathon (one full day). The public debut at scale.
- **April/May:** High frequency month — 4-5 hackathons including bring-your-own-startup format and coworking events.
- **Mid-year 2026:** The biggest hackathon the Netherlands has ever seen.

**The record to beat:** Dutch Blockchain Hackathon (DutchChain) 2019 — 700+ participants in Groningen over 4 days, 63 prototypes. If TAG does 750-1000+ in a single day focused on AI/building in Amsterdam, that's a fundamentally different statement.

## Partner strategy (phased)
- **Now (30 builders):** Focus inward. Tighten culture, run hackathons, document everything. Keep Techleap/Norsken happy. Corporate hackathons through Lerai/TAG.
- **50-80 builders:** Unofficial tool partnerships — use tools, build cool shit, tag companies on social. Let them notice you.
- **80-100+ builders:** Formal outreach to Tier 2 tool partners (Vercel, Supabase, Anthropic, Cursor, Cloudflare etc.) from position of strength with content trail proving community value.

Partner tiers:
1. **Institutional** (Techleap, Norsken, Rockstart, EIT Digital) — talent pipeline pitch
2. **Tool/platform** (Vercel, Supabase, Anthropic, Cursor) — community distribution in Europe
3. **Corporate** (UMC Utrecht, OHRA type) — access to builder talent for projects/innovation
